## Introduction: Your Führerausweis Is Just the Beginning

Your Führerausweis confirms that you know the rules and can operate a vehicle. Becoming a **truly good driver** requires experience, self-reflection, and continuous personal development.

## Self-Awareness: You're Not Just Steering the Car — You're Steering Yourself

Behind the wheel, you show up with all your characteristics. The basics from driving school (mirror–indicator–shoulder check, glancing at the instrument panel) aren't forgotten — but what matters most is awareness of how your abilities and attitudes affect **road safety**.

## Abilities Change — Assess Them Honestly

A medical certificate is a snapshot, not a guarantee. Sleep, fatigue, illness, medication, alcohol/caffeine, or eating too much all affect perception and reaction time. If you notice you're not fit to drive, **don't get in the car** — take a taxi or public transport instead.

## Age & Health: React in Time

Declining vision or hearing, new therapies, or changed prescriptions require adjustments (glasses, hearing aids) and, when in doubt, a conversation with your doctor: "Is it still safe for me to drive under these new circumstances?" Acting responsibly protects you and others.

## Attitudes: The Foundation of Good Driving

Knowing the rules is mandatory; **respecting them** is a matter of attitude. Overtaking across a solid line, ignoring road signs, or exceeding speed limits isn't a "loophole" — it's a risk to human lives. Consideration for pedestrians and other road users is part of **road culture**.

## Temperament & Ethics: Character at the Wheel

Recognise your behaviour under pressure (impatience, impulsivity) and build in routines that calm you down. Ethics and responsibility in traffic are part of your personality — **consistently following rules** matters more than "perfect" vehicle control.

## Quick Pre-Drive Check

- Am I rested and focused?
- Is anything affecting my reaction time (medication, alcohol, illness)?
- Is my equipment correct (glasses/hearing aid)?
- Am I ready to follow the rules **without exception**?

## Conclusion

Working on yourself is part of driving. **Self-awareness + good habits + attitude** = a driver who truly deserves the label "good" — visible in everyday life, not just in the exam.
