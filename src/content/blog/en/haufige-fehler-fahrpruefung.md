A calm head and clean basics count more than perfection. This guide covers the typical stumbling blocks on exam day — plus concrete countermeasures so you can navigate them with confidence.

## Observation & Eye Scanning

**Mistake:** Checking mirrors too infrequently, missing the shoulder check when changing lanes or turning.

**How to avoid it:** Mirror scan every 5–8 seconds. Before you steer: Mirror → Indicator → Shoulder check → Steer.

## Speed Adaptation

**Mistake:** Driving too fast in 30 km/h zones or too slowly on main roads.

**Tip:** "Read" the environment (school, construction site, weather) + check the speedometer. Goal: smooth, but rule-compliant.

## Right of Way & Intersections

**Mistake:** Uncertainty when entering intersections, failing to look left and right.

**Tip:** Reduce speed before every intersection, actively look right and left, and apply right-of-way rules consistently — even when the road looks empty.

## Merging & Exiting (Roundabouts, Motorways)

**Mistake:** Hesitating when merging, indicating too late or not at all.

**Tip:** Plan the gap early, accelerate rather than brake, indicate in good time, don't forget the shoulder check.

## Parking & Manoeuvring

**Mistake:** Rushing, touching the kerb, incorrect steering angle.

**Tip:** Use fixed reference points, roll slowly, correcting is allowed. Safety > speed.

## Technical Questions (Vehicle Check)

**Mistake:** No idea about lights, tyres, or fluids.

**Tip:** 10-minute pre-exam checklist: lights/signals, tyre pressure/tread, windscreen washer fluid, hazard lights, fog light.

## Managing Nerves

**Mistake:** Rushed decisions, "tunnel vision".

**Tip:** Box breathing (4–4–4–4), inner checklist, keep driving after a mistake — composure is what counts.
